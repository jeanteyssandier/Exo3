Transit-Timing Variation Signature of Planet Migration:

The Case of K2-24

Jean Teyssandier & Anne-Sophie Libert (naXys, University of Namur)

Abstract
The convergent migration of two planets in a gaseous disc can lead to capture in mean motion resonance (MMR). We study the case of K2-24, a system of two planets whose period ratio indicates that they are in or near the 2:1 MMR, with non-zero eccentricities and large-amplitude transit-timing variations (TTV). By means of N-body simulations, we explore a scenario that can explain the architecture of this system. We propose a model in which capture in resonance occurs during migration in a disc with strong eccentricity damping, and is followed by eccentricity excitation during the dispersal of the disc, assisted by a third planet whose presence has been suggested by radial velocity observations. This model can account for the eccentricities of the two planets and their period ratio, as well as allowing us to reproduce the amplitude and period of the TTVs. It allows for a unified view of the formation and evolution history of K2-24, from disc-induced migration to its currently observed properties.
